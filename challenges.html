Challenges & Barriers

We discussed circumstances and perceptions that can stand in the way of people finding and making use of reentry resources. Identifying these barriers can help us craft the site and the content in a way that supports distributing information in the guide creatively to overcome these barriers.

  • Incarcerated people are often dealing with fear.
    • For their physical safety (or as a residual anxiety, once released)
    • Fear of how their future will be affected
    • The community's fear of their reintegration into the neighborhood, if the family or the neighborhood has achieved upward mobility
  • Competing priorities
    • Releasing stress through physical activity can take precedence over taking advantage of education opportunities in prison.]]
    • Trouble envisioning how school relates to life after prison
  • Lack of internet access
    • No internet available in prison
    • Limited data plans
    • Ability to afford internet access, in general
  • Lack of support from counselors or family
  • Anxiety about their upcoming release, self doubt
  • Overconfidence, lack of awareness of timelines for seeking resources
  • Temptation to commit crimes after release
  • Trying to provide for a new family or unplanned pregancy
  • Stigma associated with mental health support for:
    • Domestic violence
    • Anger management
    • Substance abuse
