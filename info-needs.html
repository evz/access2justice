Information needs

Reader goals

We have identified the following use cases, or scenarios in which the guide would be useful, for the reentry guide:

  • Finding general information on reentry resources – broad searches
  • Finding content within the the guide – specific searches
    • Finding housing
    • Finding education resources
    • Finding employment resources
    • Finding health care
  • Sharing information from the guide
  • Making a plan for reentry
  • Providing feedback on guide content
  • Requesting a copy of the guide

Stress cases

We have defined some uses cases in the form of stress cases. The benefit of stress cases is in considering realistic, important uses that require the guide to meet the challenges of a stressful situation. If readers can find and share the information they need in these circumstances, then the guide will perform that much better in less stressful cases.

  • My record is keeping me from finding:
  • A job
  • Housing
  • If I don’t find a place to live, I won’t be released from prison.
  • I need a place to live where my children can also stay with me.
  • I’m worried about regaining custody of my children.
  • I need a GED to get the job I want.
  • I need a certification to get the job I want, and I'm worried I can't afford the classes.
  • As a sex offender, I can only live certain places.
  • As a sex offender, I am only eligible for some housing and employment programs.
  • I don’t have an ID or a social security card.
  • I access the internet on my cell phone and I can’t afford to go over my data limit.
  • I don't have any way to access the internet to find resources.
  • I can only ask so much from my family. I can’t be a burden on them.
  • I need a place to stay tonight.
  • I can't afford groceries this week.
  • I need to have a place to live so I don’t violate my parole.
  • I need to show I’ve been looking for a job so I don’t violate my parole.
  • I'm worried I may have violated parole.
  • I want to find “felon-friendly” employers.
  • I need to find resources close to me, because:
  • I use public transit, or I catch rides with friends and family.
  • I need to be home by curfew.
  • I have to get to and from work on time.
  • I'm worried I can't afford substance abuse help.
  • I'm worried I can't afford health care to manage my:
  • Chronic health condition.
  • Pregnancy
  • Birth control.
